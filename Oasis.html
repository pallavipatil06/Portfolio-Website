Oasis

  • Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Platforms: Android and iOS
  • Tools: Sketch,InVision and Photoshop

Project Overview: Have you ever come across an empty space or corner in a house and thought which plant will look good? This app helps users to design their space with house plants.

Contex: purpose of this project was to design a native mobile app for both iOS and Android versions following guidelines.

Objective: In the Market, there are a lot of Interior, Landscaping, Space decorating apps. Few apps concentrate on just indoor plants and ways to decorate houses using them. Plants are as important as other accessories in space. Users here will not only learn about plants and their benefits but will also be able to visualize how to decorate a house using them. Indoor plants do not just add decor elements but they help to boost air quality and even reduce stress.

My Design Process

Discovery

Learning From Others

While doing research, I found two apps which are similar to my native app” Oasis”. From Plant, Life and Balance App I learned high-quality images and minimalistic looks attracts users. Providing written instructions while performing action makes it easy for a user to learn how to use the functions. Another App Nature Id has beautiful sections of Blog from the user and trending post. I like the idea about a blog post from a designer, tips, care, and benefits of indoor plants can guide the user to decor their house.

Define

User

This app is for nature lovers & users who are interested in decorating his house by using plants.

Key App Feautures

  • Browse different blogs, recommended posts, and articles from different users and designers.Different decorating styles can be explored.
  • Different decorating styles can be explored.
  • Explore different indoor plants, select and then try to visualize in their space.
  • Save their preferences in their wishlist by login into their account. Different decorating styles can be explored.

Userflow

Ideate

Wireframes/Low Fidelity Frames

Based on User Flow, I sketched layouts using “Crazy 8” technique. I refined them to get a better idea.

Mid Fidelity Frames

The next step, after the Low fidelity frame was to transfer them to a digitalized Mid-fidelity frame. These were created by using Material guidelines for android and Human Interface Guidelines for iOS.

Prototype

High-Fidelity frames were created using high-quality imagery, color palette, and respective typography.

Testing

Now after styling prototypes, it was time to evaluate the adequacy of the design solution. To determine whether this design is working or needs iteration, feedback from the users was required. The above prototypes were shared with 5 users to test the design. Feedback from ( 3 Android and 2 iOS ) users was received which was very helpful for iterating the design. I took note of feedback and the following changes were made in the design.

Challenges

The most difficult part was to understand both platform's respective guidelines and incorporate them in design. Material guidelines were easy to source and had extensive detailed templates. Being an Android user this was easy to understand but the challenging part was to source iOS templates. The written information of the Human interface is more but templates for different scenarios were less. Buttons and tabs difference in iOS took a while for me to understand. Another challenge was trying to keep both designs similar but still following respective guidelines. Like rounding the corners of images for iOS and keeping straight for android.

UI Design

Take Aways

I would like to carry out user interviews and understand more in detail what users would like additional features. Due to the limited timeline, I directly jumped to design based on some research. As per Project requirements, we had to only design for MVP but definitely would like to add some additional features as the nearest nursery to source plants or showing rating features which calculate air quality of room based on the number of plants.
